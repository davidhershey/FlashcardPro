FlashcardPro

EECS493 Winter 2015

Designed For Mark Ackerman

Designed By Jared DeFrain, Sam Devaprasad, David Hershey, and Lauren Molley


We created a flashcard application where users can import and create sets of flashcards useful for studying purposes. The idea came to one of our team members while studying for Professor Ackerman's EECS493 midterm exam. Buying physical notecards to review vocabulary can be a pain and usually are hard to make copies. Our application digitized the notecard-making process and also allows people to import and export other peoples notecard sets.


The guts of our project gives the user the ability to create a set of flashcards, study flashcards, and share your flashcards with other users. We also added additional deck scores to keep track of students progress while trying to prioritize which notecards to study.


We also utilized Leitner system to enhance the learning process. The Leitner system is an algorithm that determines the optimal order of displaying flash cards to encourage information retention. This algorithm has been shown to be extremely effective, and by trying to make it more understandable and easier to use we could help people memorize terms much more effectively.


As shown in the slideshow below we made this application using Qt. Qt had all of the necessary resources needed to develop and test our application on.
